About HausaNLP

Pioneering natural language processing research for the Hausa language, connecting technology with culture and community.

Our Mission

To develop comprehensive, culturally-aware natural language processing tools and resources for the Hausa language that preserve linguistic heritage while enabling modern technological advancement. We strive to make AI technology accessible and relevant to Hausa-speaking communities across West Africa and the diaspora.

Our Vision

A future where Hausa speakers can fully participate in the digital age with AI systems that understand their language, culture, and context. We envision breaking down language barriers and ensuring that technological progress includes and empowers African language communities.

Our Approach

HausaNLP combines traditional linguistic expertise with cutting-edge machine learning techniques. Our interdisciplinary team brings together computational linguists, AI researchers, and native Hausa speakers to ensure our solutions are both technically advanced and culturally authentic.

Community-Centered

We work directly with Hausa-speaking communities to understand their needs and ensure our research has real-world impact.

Culturally Aware

Our models are designed to understand cultural context, idioms, and the rich literary tradition of the Hausa language.

Collaborative

We believe in open research and actively collaborate with institutions across Africa and globally to advance low-resource NLP.

Sustainable

Our solutions are designed to be computationally efficient and accessible, ensuring long-term sustainability and adoption.

Ethical

We prioritize data privacy, linguistic rights, and responsible AI development in all our research endeavors.

Inclusive

Our team reflects the diversity of Hausa speakers, including various dialects and regional variations.

Our Journey

Founded in 2023

HausaNLP was established at Bayero University, Kano, recognizing the critical need for advanced language technology resources for Hausa, one of Africa's most widely spoken languages with over 70 million speakers worldwide.

Key Achievements

  • Developed the first large-scale Hausa language corpus with over 10 million tokens
  • Published breakthrough research on Hausa morphological analysis
  • Created educational partnerships with schools across northern Nigeria
  • Launched open-source tools used by researchers globally
  • Established collaborations with major tech companies for resource development

Looking Forward

As we continue to grow, we're expanding our research scope to include speech technologies, multimodal AI, and applications in education, healthcare, and governance. Our goal is to make HausaNLP the leading center for African language AI research.
