About FUT Minna AI Lab

Advancing AI and language technologies for low-resource and multilingual African languages—starting from North-Central Nigeria.

Our Mission

To build practical and research-grade language technologies for low-resource African languages by creating high-quality corpora (parallel, monolingual, and speech datasets) and developing robust models for machine translation (MT), automatic speech recognition (ASR), and code-switching. Our core focus includes Nupe and its endangered dialects (such as Dibo and Kakanda), as well as Gbagyi and Hausa code-switching.

Our Vision

A future where speakers of North-Central Nigerian languages can fully participate in the digital world through AI systems that understand their languages, dialects, and speech. We envision a strong ecosystem of open datasets, tools, and models that support education, inclusion, and long-term language preservation—especially for endangered dialect communities.

Our Approach

FUT Minna AI Lab brings together language expertise, community participation, and modern machine learning. We prioritize data quality, reproducibility, and culturally grounded research—because low-resource language technology succeeds when it is built with speakers, not just for speakers.

Community-Driven

We collaborate with native speakers and communities to create meaningful datasets and evaluate real-world usefulness.

Multilingual by Design

We work across languages and dialects—Nupe, Gbagyi, Hausa code-switching—and support multilingual African NLP research.

Open & Collaborative

We share resources (where ethically permitted) and collaborate with researchers and institutions to strengthen African language AI.

Data First

We focus on corpus creation: monolingual and parallel text, speech recordings, and ASR-ready datasets for low-resource languages.

Ethical & Responsible

We prioritize consent, privacy, community benefit, and respectful use of language data—especially for endangered dialect speakers.

Inclusive Dialect Coverage

We recognize language diversity and support endangered dialects such as Dibo and Kakanda alongside broader Nupe resources.

Our Journey

Built from a Need

FUT Minna AI Lab was formed to address a major gap: many Nigerian languages—especially in North-Central Nigeria—remain underrepresented in mainstream NLP research. Our work focuses on building foundational resources and models that can power translation, speech technology, and language access for both research and community impact.

Core Research Themes

  • Corpus Creation: parallel corpora, monolingual corpora, and speech corpora for low-resource languages
  • Machine Translation: MT for Nupe (and related language varieties)
  • ASR: speech recognition for Nupe and its endangered dialects, and other local languages where feasible
  • Code-Switching: Hausa code-switching data and models (text and/or speech)
  • Dialect Preservation: documentation and datasets for endangered Nupe dialects such as Dibo and Kakanda
  • Multilingual African NLP: evaluation, benchmarking, and low-resource methods

Looking Forward

We are expanding our datasets and models for North-Central Nigerian languages and building tools that can be used by researchers, educators, and communities. Our long-term goal is to grow a sustainable pipeline—from data collection and annotation to model training and deployment—focused on low-resource and endangered African languages.
