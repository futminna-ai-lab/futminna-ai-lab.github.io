Research & Development

FUT Minna AI Lab advances language technologies for extremely low-resource and endangered languages, with a strong focus on North-Central Nigerian languages (Nupe and its dialects, Gbagyi) and code-switching.

Our Research Areas

We combine linguistics, community-driven data creation, and modern machine learning to build practical, open resources for text and speech—so these languages can be supported in education, governance, health, and daily life.

Machine Translation (MT)

We build Nupe–English MT systems (including LLM-based approaches) and create parallel datasets to improve translation quality for low-resource language pairs.

Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR)

We develop ASR-ready speech datasets and baselines for Nupe and Gbagyi, with attention to real recording conditions and endangered Nupe dialects (e.g., Dibo, Kakanda).

Corpus Creation (Text & Speech)

We build monolingual and parallel corpora, plus curated speech corpora for training and evaluation—designed for reproducible research and community benefit.

Code-Switching & Multilingual NLP

We study Hausa–English code-switching and develop datasets and evaluation setups that reflect how people actually communicate in mixed-language settings.

Evaluation & Cultural Relevance

Beyond automatic metrics, we emphasize human evaluation and cultural adequacy—ensuring translations and transcriptions preserve meaning and local context.

Open Tools & Deployment

We package datasets, scripts, and baselines to help researchers and practitioners build usable systems— and we prioritize open, well-documented releases.

Nupe Machine Translation (NupeMT)

We develop Nupe–English neural and LLM-based MT systems, supported by parallel corpora and careful evaluation. Our work aims to enable practical translation tools for Nupe-speaking communities and support research on extremely low-resource MT.

Speech Data for ASR (Nupe & Gbagyi)

We collect and release speech data for ASR, including a Nupe corpus (~5 hours) and a Gbagyi corpus (~7 hours) supported by Naija Voices microgrants. These datasets provide building blocks for robust ASR baselines.

  • Access Datasets

Hausa–English Code-Switching

We create code-switched datasets and explore methods for language identification, MT robustness, and evaluation in realistic multilingual communication. This supports NLP systems that work in the way people actually speak and write.
