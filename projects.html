Our Projects

Explore our projects advancing low-resource NLP, machine translation, and speech technologies for Endangered Nigerian languages (Nupe and its endangered dialects, Gbagyi) and code-switching.

Nupe–English Parallel Corpus (Machine Translation)

We develop and release parallel corpora to support machine translation (MT) and multilingual NLP for Nupe. This dataset supports baseline MT systems, evaluation, and future LLM-based translation work.

Dataset: Mendeley Data (V1)

Related Publications

Hausa–English Code-Switching Dataset

We build code-switching resources to support research on multilingual communication, language identification, and robust MT/ASR evaluation in realistic mixed-language settings.

Dataset: Mendeley Data (V1)

Related Publications

Nupe Speech Data for ASR (Naija Voices Microgrant)

We create speech corpora and ASR-ready datasets for Nupe, including work towards coverage of endangered dialects such as Dibo and Kakanda. Our focus is building resources that support training, evaluation, and community use.

Dataset: Mozilla Data Collective

ASR Research

Gbagyi Speech Data for ASR (Naija Voices Microgrant)

We build speech datasets for under-resourced languages like Gbagyi to enable automatic speech recognition and inclusive language technologies for North-Central Nigeria.

Dataset: Mozilla Data Collective

ASR Research

NupeMT-7B: LLM-based Machine Translation

We explore LLM-based MT by fine-tuning a 7B-parameter model for Nupe translation tasks. This project aims to improve translation quality for low-resource languages and support practical translation tools for Nupe-speaking communities.

If you would like access to the model, training details, or evaluation results, please contact us.
